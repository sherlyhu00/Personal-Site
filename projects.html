Integrating Travel Impedance for Wheelchair Users Transfer Route Planning

Atlanta | Supervised by Dr. Randall Guensler

This ongoing research focuses on improving public transit accessibility for wheelchair users through advanced route planning methodologies.

  • Designed sidewalk-to-bus transfer link impedance methodology following additive impedance factor approach for wheelchair user bus stop access route planning
  • Identified various environmental elements that impact travel experience of wheelchair users, ensuring accessibility of bus stop access routes
  • Developed multi-mode transit simulation model for wheelchair users based on TransitSim4 model framework, focusing on bus transfer trips
Accessibility Planning Transit Modeling Python GIS

Impact of Muni Metro Transit Station Location on Public Transportation Accessibility

Atlanta | Supervised by Dr. Rama Sivakumar

Comprehensive analysis of transit accessibility in San Francisco using real-time data and advanced spatial modeling techniques.

  • Integrated real-time GTFS data for travel time and transit station location index development, extracting from 511SF Bay API to analyze vehicle positions and estimated delays
  • Applied logistic decay regression to model transit demand probabilities
  • Extracted transfer distributions from over 1,500 OD pairs and matched them with trip IDs to identify most likely transfer locations
  • Created comprehensive accessibility visualizations through ArcGIS Pro and ArcGIS Story Map, mapping spatial distribution of accessibility scores across zones
Transit Analysis GTFS Data ArcGIS Pro Python Data Visualization
