May 2024 - Jul 2024

Transfer Location Impact on Public Transit Accessibility

San Francisco, CA | Applied Research Project

Comprehensive analysis examining how transfer station locations influence public transit accessibility in San Francisco's Muni Metro network, incorporating both travel time and transfer location components.

  • Developed novel accessibility index incorporating transfer location probability and travel time decay functions to evaluate 113 Muni Metro stations across San Francisco
  • Generated and analyzed 1,500 origin-destination pairs using GTFS data, identifying 79.2% direct trips and 20.8% transfer-required journeys
  • Revealed accessibility improvements in northeastern areas (Union Square, Twin Peaks, Gate Park) when transfer locations are included in traditional travel-time-only models
  • Demonstrated that grid-like transit networks with distributed transfer stations enhance accessibility for areas distant from city centers, particularly during peak hours
